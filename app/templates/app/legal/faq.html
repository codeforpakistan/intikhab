Voting Process

How do I vote in an election?

Log in to your account, navigate to the active election, review candidates and their information, then cast your vote securely. The system will confirm your vote submission.

Can I vote multiple times?

No, each user can only vote once per election. The system prevents duplicate voting to maintain election integrity.

When can I vote?

You can only vote during the official voting period for each election. Check the election details for start and end times.

Can I change my vote after submission?

No, votes are final once submitted to ensure election integrity. Please review all information carefully before confirming your vote.

What if I make a mistake while voting?

The system will show you a confirmation screen before final submission. If you notice an error, you can go back and change your selection before confirming.

Do I get a receipt for my vote?

Yes, the system provides a confirmation that your vote was successfully recorded, but without revealing your specific choice to maintain privacy.

Security & Privacy

Is my vote secret and secure?

Yes, we use advanced encryption to ensure your vote is completely anonymous and secure. No one can trace your vote back to you. See privacy policy

How is my personal information protected?

We follow strict data protection protocols and only collect necessary information for voter verification. All data is encrypted and securely stored.

Can administrators see how I voted?

No, even system administrators cannot see individual voting choices. The encryption ensures complete voter privacy.

How do you prevent election fraud?

We use multiple security measures including voter authentication, encryption, audit trails, and prevention of duplicate voting.

Are the election results verifiable?

Yes, the system provides verification tools that allow independent verification of results without compromising voter privacy.

What happens to my data after the election?

Personal data is retained only as long as necessary for legal and audit requirements, then securely deleted according to our data retention policy.

Technical Support

What if I'm having technical issues?

Try refreshing your browser, clearing your browser cache, or using a different browser. If issues persist, contact us immediately.

Which browsers are supported?

Intikhab works with all modern browsers including Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge. Make sure you're using the latest version.

Can I vote on mobile devices?

Yes, the platform is fully responsive and works on smartphones and tablets. We recommend using the latest version of your mobile browser.

What if the system is down during voting?

We have backup systems and monitoring in place. If you encounter issues during critical voting periods, contact our support team immediately.

Do I need to install any software?

No, Intikhab is entirely web-based. You only need a modern browser and internet connection to participate in elections.

What if I forgot my login credentials?

Use the password reset feature on the login page, or contact support if you need help recovering your account.

Account & Access

Who can create an account?

Account creation is typically managed by election administrators. Contact your organization's election officials for access.

How do I update my profile information?

Visit your profile page after logging in to update your personal information, but note that some election-critical data may be locked during active voting periods.

What if I can't access my account?

Try resetting your password first. If that doesn't work, contact support with your account details for assistance.

Can I delete my account?

Account deletion may be restricted during active elections. Contact support to discuss account removal options and data deletion.

Why can't I see certain elections?

Elections may be restricted to specific groups or user categories. You can only participate in elections where you're an eligible voter.

How do I know if I'm eligible to vote?

Eligible elections will appear in your elections list. If you believe you should have access to an election, contact the election administrators.
